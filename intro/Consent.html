You are invited to take part in an economic experiment studying trading behaviour in financial markets. This project is funded by the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada. You will be asked to trade stocks on a virtual financial market with other participants.

Your participation in this study is entirely voluntary and presents no known risks or burdens. You will receive a fixed payment for participating, as well as additional compensation based on your trading performance and your answers to a financial literacy quiz. You can withdraw from the study at any time without penalty. However, if you withdraw before the end of the experiment, you will receive only a partial payment reflecting the time spent.

All data generated during this study will remain confidential. You will be asked to provide some basic demographic information, but none of this information will be used to personally identify you and no personal information will appear in any published study. Your decision to participate will remain completely confidential and only the researchers will have access to individual data. We do not collect any data that can personally identify you.

Data will be stored electronically on a secure server accessible only by the research team using a secure password. Data will be retained in de-identifiable format for 7 years and discarded afterwards, in accordance with standard practices of the American Psychological Association. As required by the guidelines of the American Economic Association, during the publication process the data may be shared with other investigators for re-analysis. However, only details sufficient to permit replication will be shared, ensuring security and confidentiality.

This study has been reviewed by the Research Ethics Board at the University of Calgary. The research study you are participating in may be reviewed for quality assurance to ensure that required laws and guidelines are followed. If chosen, representative(s) of the Human Research Ethics Program (HREP) may access study-related data and/or consent materials as part of the review. All information accessed by the HREP will be upheld to the same level of confidentiality stated by the research team.

If you are interested, you can receive a summary of the research study by emailing the primary researchers.

  • Prof. Marius Zoican at marius.zoican@ucalgary.ca
  • Dr. Philipp Chapkovski at chapkovski@gmail.com

If you wish to obtain further information regarding your rights as a participant, you may contact the Conjoint Faculties Research Ethics Board (CFREB) at the University of Calgary at cfreb@ucalgary.ca or (403) 220-8640.

By proceeding, I agree to participate in research. I understand that I can withdraw my consent to participate at any time and that by giving consent I am not giving up any of my legal rights.
